Colors and Contrast

Guidelines

Web Content Accessibility Guidelines 2 (WCAG 2) defines contrast as the "difference in perceived luminence or brightness between two colors"1. The difference is expressed as a ratio ranging from 1:1, which is white on white, to 21:1 which is black on white.

An instructional designer will want to me the minimum Level AA requirement. To do This the ratio must be 4.5:1. There are 3 exceptions:

  1. Large Text: 3:1
  2. Decorative Text: no requirement
  3. Logos and Brandnames: no requirement

You can improved perceived contrast by using halo or outline with text. The title in This document uses creates an outline effect with color and the new CSS text-stroke property. Disabilities and Color

Contrast can help anybody whether they suffer from disabilities or are just trying to achieve the best reading experience. There are diabilities that allow students to pick up visual content without a screen reader, but who can benefit from thoughtful consideration of color.

About 300 million people worldwide suffer from some form of color blindness and 99% of these people are red/green blind. The image below summarizes different types of color blindness showing how different colors are percieved.1

Many times designers will use the color red to alert to an error or stop and action, and the color green will be used to encourage or signal success, you can see that some students will see these color as very similar. Alternative to color for providing visual cues include using textures, animation and icons.
